10 Important Actions for Cybersecurity

Protect yourself and your data from cyber threats.

1

Use Strong, Unique Passwords and a Password Manager

Create complex passwords using a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid reusing passwords across different accounts. Utilize a reputable password manager to securely store and generate strong, unique passwords for all your online services.

2

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Add an extra layer of security to your accounts by enabling MFA wherever possible. This typically requires a second form of verification, such as a code from your phone, a fingerprint, or a physical security key, in addition to your password.

3

Keep Software and Operating Systems Updated

Regularly update your operating system, web browsers, antivirus software, and all other applications. Updates often include critical security patches that fix vulnerabilities attackers could exploit. Enable automatic updates if available.

4

Be Wary of Phishing Scams

Exercise caution with unsolicited emails, messages, or phone calls that ask for personal information or prompt you to click on suspicious links or open attachments. Always verify the sender's legitimacy before interacting. When in doubt, delete it.

5

Back Up Your Data Regularly

Routinely back up your important files and data to an external hard drive or a secure cloud storage service. This protects you from data loss due to hardware failure, accidental deletion, or ransomware attacks.

6

Use Antivirus/Anti-Malware Software

Install and maintain reputable antivirus and anti-malware software on all your devices. Keep it updated and run regular scans to detect and remove malicious software that could compromise your system.

7

Secure Your Home Wi-Fi Network

Change the default password of your router to a strong, unique one. Enable WPA2 or WPA3 encryption. Consider using a VPN (Virtual Private Network) for an added layer of security, especially when using public Wi-Fi.

8

Be Careful What You Share Online

Think twice before sharing personal information on social media or other public platforms. Cybercriminals can use this information for identity theft, targeted phishing attacks, or social engineering schemes.

9

Lock Your Devices

Always lock your computer, smartphone, and tablet when you step away from them. Use strong PINs, passwords, or biometric authentication (fingerprint/face ID) to prevent unauthorized access.

10

Monitor Your Accounts and Statements

Regularly check your bank statements, credit card reports, and online account activity for any suspicious or unauthorized transactions. Report any discrepancies immediately to the relevant financial institution or service provider.
